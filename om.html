Om Mobile App Prototype

An app to help coffee lovers discover cafes and coffee shops that best fit their preferences and place orders for menu items.

3

01. Brand Philosophy

Meditation is proven to do a lot for the human body spiritually, emotionally, and mentally. In today’s world, it’s easy for many to get caught in the stress of daily life. This is where Om comes in; this app brings the yoga studio to you, wherever you are, to provide you with a high-quality meditation experience. We understand knowing where to start can be a major roadblock for those new to meditation, so Om is tailored to offer meditation classes for all levels.

Our Goals for the App

  • Make meditation a regular practice in users’ lives
  • Become a social hub for anyone interested in wellness and meditation practices
  • Partner with local businesses to promote their meditation and wellness classes while increasing our brand awareness

02. Where Do We Start?

We started to scope out our competition and created a competitive analysis to get a better understanding of the current meditation app market. Our main competitor would be Headspace, another app centered around instructor-led guided meditation. They're recognized as one of the leading mindfulness and meditation apps out on the market, so we started to think about how to differentiate our brand from the competition.

Next, we interviewed six people, mostly young adults in their 20's-30's, and conducted a survey to get a gauge on what users are looking for when they’re using a meditation app. We found that our interviewees struggled with finding the time and motivation for meditation, but were mostly open to incorporating it in their lives to be more present amidst their busy lives.

We also created a mood board to get our ideas organized. Interestingly, our final product turned out to be much different than where our initial thought process started.


03. Ideation

To ideate potential features for CafeHunt, we decided to go through the “I Like, I Wish, What If?” brainstorming process. After this, we determined the importance and feasibility of these possibilities to narrow our scope. In the interest of time (this project had a 2-week deadline), we decided to implement three key features in our prototype:

  • Search/filter system for finding coffee shops based on user preferences
  • Display information about shops, including location, reviews, and menu
  • Simple online ordering system

We created a user flow of the app that details the onboarding process, searching for coffee shops, and placing an order for pickup at a selected shop. Using this as our guide, we began work on the prototype.

User Testing

We tested each of our paper prototypes with 3 people each. After figuring out that the Prototype #1 covered the issues found in the Prototype #2, we decided to go with #1 and solve the issues found in this. From the feedback given by our user testers, we decided to make the following changes going into the low-fidelity prototype:

  • Make buttons/navigation elements more visible using size, color, etc.
  • Animated transitions between screens can be less jarring (i.e. slower) in some parts to give users time to read and process screens
